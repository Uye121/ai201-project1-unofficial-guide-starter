[IS UCSD SOCIALLY DEAD?]
To address the elephant in the room — what's the social life like, is UCSD really socially dead? No, no, no, I don't think it is. At UCSD you just have to kind of go out of your way to be social. You'll meet people in the dorms, but once classes start and midterms start rolling around, people get pretty antisocial. So there are a few things you can do about it.

---

[HOW TO MEET PEOPLE & MAKE FRIENDS]
Don't totally rely on meeting people in classes — generally in classes people don't like to talk to each other, and some people don't even show up, so don't rely on that. What worked for me was just making friends with the people on your floor, your dorm, and your roommates, and then you expand from there once you have a solid base of friends to go get food and stuff with. Because you're all taking the same GEs, you'll be in a lot of the same classes, so that's where a lot of the bonding comes from.

---

[GREEK LIFE — RUSH & RECRUITMENT TIMING]
One option is Greek life. Sorority formal recruitment is week one of fall quarter, and some will also do informal recruitment in the winter and spring — but not every sorority, some only recruit week one of fall. It's extremely formal. For guys there's fraternity rush (same thing, they just call it "rush" because it takes place over one week and you feel rushed). Official fraternity rush is week two of both fall quarter and spring quarter, and some fraternities might do an informal rush winter quarter, but most don't. So if you're planning on rushing: go for fall if you want to jump right in, or wait until spring if you want to see what college is like without a fraternity first.

---

[CLUBS & SPORTS TEAMS]
Clubs and sports teams typically recruit in the first two weeks. If you want to join a club team, a club sports team, or any of the many other clubs, you can read about them on UCSD's student website. That's another good way to meet people, in addition to the people you'll meet in the dorms.

---

[THE COLLEGE SYSTEM — SOCIAL SIDE]
UCSD is divided into six colleges, sort of like the houses of Hogwarts: Revelle, Thurgood Marshall, Earl Warren, John Muir, Eleanor Roosevelt, and Sixth (with Seventh being developed). You live with everyone from your college since they're separated into sections across campus — one corner is ERC, one corner is Muir, one corner is Warren, etc. You'll see your college-mates around a lot and you all take the same GEs together, then take your major classes with people from other colleges, so you get a nice diversity. Roosevelt was my college and I recommend it if you want to travel and get some culture (it's focused on being a global citizen).

---

[HOUSING & ROOMMATES]
Resident life depends on the number of roommates you get. Sometimes you get lucky and get put into an apartment — though that might not actually be lucky, because you might not meet as many people. If you're in the dorms (for example in ERC) you'll most likely be in a triple with two roommates, unless you apply for a single. You should definitely live with a roommate, it's really fun. One thing to know: unlike some other universities and the UK, UCSD suites are not co-ed — guys live with guys and girls live with girls in a suite, though they can live on the same floor, just not in the same suite or apartment.

---

[DINING & CAMPUS FOOD SPOTS]
The dining halls are like any mall food — not super amazing, not gourmet. One thing to know is that UCSD charges per item, not per swipe like other universities, so you pay for each item and can leave with it. If you live off campus, I highly suggest you don't get a dining plan — it's much cheaper to cook for yourself. There are two campus currencies: Triton Cash (like a gift card, accepted almost everywhere on campus) and Dining Dollars (the meal plan, only usable at dining halls). Note that Price Center does not accept Dining Dollars, only Triton Cash. Price Center is where Panda Express, Subway, and Starbucks are — you can get those EE sandwiches (not that good) but the Panda hits different.
